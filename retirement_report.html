ℹ️ How to read this analysis

What is Monte Carlo analysis? Your deterministic plan uses a single average return each year. In reality, markets are volatile — some years gain 25%, others lose 20%. Monte Carlo runs 1,000+ simulations, each with randomly varied annual returns drawn from a normal distribution centered on your target return. This stress-tests your plan against sequence-of-returns risk (bad years early in retirement are most damaging).

The Survival Curve (main chart): The X-axis is your age; the Y-axis is the percentage of simulations where your portfolio still has money at that age. For example, if the curve shows 82% at age 85, it means 820 out of 1,000 scenarios still had a positive balance at that age.

🟢 >80% — Safe Zone 🟡 50–80% — Caution Zone 🔴 <50% — At Risk Zone

Ending Wealth Percentiles: After all simulations complete, the 10th/50th/90th percentile values show the range of portfolio outcomes at your life expectancy. The 10th percentile represents a "bad luck" scenario; the 90th represents favorable returns throughout retirement.

Volatility input: Annual standard deviation (σ). A diversified 80/20 stock-bond portfolio typically has σ ≈ 10–14%. Higher σ = wider range of outcomes but same average — it spreads the survival curve rather than shifting it.
